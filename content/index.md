## The Known World of Erodas

[[Erodas]] is a living world — two moons overhead, four seasons, and a history older than memory. Below are the continents currently charted by the Atlasing Society. Some are well-documented; others remain at the edge of legend.

<div class="continent-grid">

<div class="continent-card">

### [[Korlornium]]
*A small, isolated continent — the heart of the campaign*

A land of theological strife, diverse ecology, and unique geological phenomena. Divided by [[The Scar]], the western forests of [[Caldrith]] are home to wealthy cities and complex factions, while the arid eastern expanse of [[Siroth]] is harsh, conservative, and deep with history. Two thousand years since [[The Great Wave]], the scars of catastrophe still shape its people.

</div>

<div class="continent-card">

### [[Dracofold]]
*Volcanic continent of draconic origin — ruled by the dragon queen*

The air above Dracofold smells of sulfur and hot stone. [[Tiamat]]'s rule is absolute here — chromatic dragons serve as her lieutenants, and humanoid populations survive under their shadow. Rogue dragons exiled from her court have spread across Erodas, settling in remote regions and establishing their own smaller tyrannies. At the continent's molten heart lies the **Cradle of Scales**, where all dragonkind is said to have been born.

</div>

<div class="continent-card">

### [[Goghdor]]
*A towering fjord — closer to the heavens than anywhere else on Erodas*

Known across Erodas as **The Reach**, Goghdor is not a continent in the traditional sense — it is a single colossal rock rising from the ocean, its peaks so high that those who live at the summit exist above the clouds. Home to the **Star Touched**, Aasimar of elven and dwarven blood whose culture is built entirely around the celestial. Every Star Touched makes the **Ascent** once in their life, climbing until the stars speak and a **Skoldrún** — a constellation spirit in animal form — manifests and stays.

</div>

<div class="continent-card">

### [[Seivara]]
*Ancient jungle continent — more legend than fact to most of Korlornium*

Sailors who have seen its coastline describe a wall of green so dense the sea appears to simply end at a forest. Seivara has no kings, no churches, and no organized religion. Authority here belongs to the **Ara** — ancient nature spirits that inhabit every river, grove, and tidal pool. The elves and pixies who call it home live in constant, careful negotiation with these spirits. The structured pantheons of Korlornium are regarded here with deep suspicion.

</div>
